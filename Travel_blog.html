Kochi

Kochi was our first stop and the perfect introduction to Kerala. The streets were filled with art galleries, spice markets, and vibrant murals. We also visited Mattancherry Palace and enjoyed shopping for souvenirs in Jew Town. Evenings were peaceful, with sea breeze and stunning golden skies.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode (Calicut) had a calm coastal charm. We visited Kozhikode Beach in the evening where the sunset painted the sky in beautiful shades of orange and pink. The local street food was unforgettable ‚Äî especially banana chips and traditional Malabar snacks. Walking along the shore with cool sea breeze and watching locals gather made the experience feel authentic and lively.

Thekkady

Thekkady was all about greenery and wildlife. We visited Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and took a peaceful boat ride on Periyar Lake, hoping to spot elephants and deer near the water. The cool climate and misty hills were a refreshing change from the coastal heat. We also explored spice plantations where we learned about cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon cultivation ‚Äî the aroma in the air was unforgettable.

Alleppey was the highlight of the trip. Staying overnight in a traditional houseboat on the backwaters was a surreal experience. As the boat slowly cruised through narrow canals lined with coconut trees, we watched village life unfold ‚Äî children waving from the banks and fishermen casting nets.
